== Description ==
<P>
**ExportFeed: List WooCommerce Products on eBay Store** plugin offers the flexibility in your hands to create and sync your product feeds from WooCommerce shop to eBay Store. Simply stating, connect your WooCommerce shop to eBay Store and list your products on eBay. Display your products on eBay to the massive number of visitors and increase your sales revenue with the help of this plugin! So, why should you use this plugin?

<h4>Create Product Feed Matching eBay Feed Specifications</h4>

When you install ExportFeed: List WooCommerce Products on eBay Store plugin, it will connect with your WooCommerce or WP eCommerce in your WordPress and grab the product information to create data feed. This reduces the manual actions required to create your product feed. 

<h4>One Plugin to Connect & Manage Multiple eBay Stores </h4> <br>
At times you may have created multiple eBay stores to sell different category of items or eBay stores on different countries but you have a common eCommerce website where you host all those products. For such situation, using this plugin will provide you with the flexibility to connect to your multiple eBay stores and create and manage feeds for them. 

<h4>Fixed & Flexible Product Category Mapping to eBay’s Category</h4><br> 
When you upload your product feed to eBay store, you need to provide category under which your product falls. To set category for your products, this plugin offers you to set the category in bulk or for individual products. Using this option, the feed file you generate will be optimized to list your products in your specified category and can grab the attention of eBay visitors.

== Installation ==

>[**HOW TO GUIDE**](http://www.exportfeed.com/tos) |||
>[** ALL FAQS **](http://www.exportfeed.com/faq/)
<P>
A few quick steps<P>
1) Upload to your WordPress website, and activate 'ExportFeed: List WooCommerce Products on eBay Store'.<BR>
2) Select 'eBay' submenu from 'ExportFeedEbay' from admin menu.<BR>
3) Select the country and click 'Connect with eBay'.<BR>
4) Once you get redirected to eBay, login with your eBay user information, read the terms and click on Agree button.<BR> 
5) You will now redirected to our website informing you about successful connection. Close the window and return to WooCommerce website.<BR>
6) Now click on 'Fetch eBay Token' button and after that your profile will be displayed in out `Accounts` Tab. Make it default.<BR>
7) Specify all the fields in 'General Setting' tab and save the settings.<BR>
8) Goto 'Create Feed' tab or 'Create New Feed' submenu of plugin.<BR>
9) Once it refreshes, Perform attribute mapping if required<BR>
10) Select your WooCommerce Product category (or all categories) from the dropdown on the right.<BR>
11) Select the corresponding category in the destination start typing to matching Categories (i.e. auto, skin care, baseball)<BR>
12) Name your feed.<BR>

<P>Click --Get Feed--
<P>
This will now download your feed you just created - ready for submission!
